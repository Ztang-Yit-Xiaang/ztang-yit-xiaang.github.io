My research focuses on the intersection of machine learning, numerical linear algebra, and physical sensing systems. I am particularly interested in developing efficient algorithms for large-scale data analysis and computational modeling of physical systems.

Current areas of interest include:

  • Randomized algorithms for large-scale matrix computations
  • Sketching and sampling methods for machine learning
  • Magnetic sensor–based pose estimation and inverse modeling
  • Data-driven modeling of mechanical and thermal systems
  • Language technologies for underrepresented languages such as Wenzhounese

Ongoing Research Projects

Randomized Sketching Algorithms for Large-Scale Linear Algebra

Independent study at the University of Minnesota exploring randomized sketching and sampling techniques for scalable machine learning and numerical linear algebra problems. Topics include leverage score sampling, CountSketch, and subspace embeddings for regression and low-rank approximation.

Magnetic Pose Estimation Using Distributed Dipole Models

Research on estimating the pose of flexible permanent magnets using magnetic sensor arrays. The work focuses on solving nonlinear inverse problems and developing algorithms for real-time shape reconstruction and visualization.

Neural HVAC Cycle Network–Enabled Optimal Control

Undergraduate research on data-driven modeling of closed-loop fluid infrastructure systems. This work combines thermal-fluid simulation with regression-based predictive control models to improve energy efficiency and system optimization.

Wenzhounese Input Method and Language Technology

Development of a digital input method for the Wenzhounese dialect aimed at supporting the preservation and computational accessibility of underrepresented languages.
